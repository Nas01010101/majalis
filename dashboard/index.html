Qwen Cloud · Track 3 — agent society · learned world model

Your agents debate too much. The world model decides when it's worth it.

A society of Qwen agents shares one belief board; two heads trained on its own logged episodes (wrong_now, superseded_next) plus a conformal threshold route debate only where the board is likely corrupted — at zero LLM calls per gate decision.

Honesty notes

  • wrong_now's 0.999 AUROC is on synthetic validation streams where weak-source displacement is highly separable; the honest generalization numbers are 0.937 AUROC on real LLM-built boards and the gate-quality figures above.
  • The offline benchmark stubs the disagreement sampler to 0 for both modes (the learned stacker measured its weight at zero; the heuristic gets its skip-path value).
  • agora-wm live cells measured at 8- and 16-step streams; 32-step pending. Hand-set-gate and single-agent numbers are frozen from the pre-learned-WM benchmark.
  • Generated 2026-07-13 by scripts/build_dashboard.py from results/ + data/ (offline eval: seeds 5000–5099, disjoint from eval 0–99, calibration 100–999, world-model training 1000–3199).
